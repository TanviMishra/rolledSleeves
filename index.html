Strike Information

This is a collection of information I heard at strike school at Lang and from the bargaining website. This is a live document that will be updated. Please reach out to me at 973.382.5712 if there is anything pertinent missing.

Information that might surprise (and enrage) you:

  1. The bargaining team have been communicating with a law firm hired by TNS. The administration isn’t even showing up at the table. It means the university would rather spend tens of thousands of dollars on outside legal counsel than agree to the raises part-time faculty deserve. [source]
  1. In the duration of bargaining, the university proposed taking away paid sick leave days which would be violating NY State Law. TNS states that they respect their part-time faculty but nothing about their bargaining process has indicated the same. Be aware of that as you read the mass emails from TNS admin. [source]
  1. part-time faculty’s current contract has loopholes that mean their health insurance can increase. In 2021, (yes, when COVID-19 spread was at its peak) TNS drastically hiked out-of-pocket medical care costs for part-time faculty.
  1. part-time faculty only get paid for contact hours aka hours they spend on campus. They don’t get paid for course prep, grading, meeting students 1-1 and much more.
  1. I make more money as a student worker (~$1000/per month) than my part-time faculty (~$800/month).

What happens in the event of a strike?

If part-time faculty strike, all classes (even online classes) taught by part-time faculty will cease for the duration of the strike. [FAQs]

What do we as TNS students do?

Strength lies in numbers and we are a LOT of people, friends.

THE BARE MINIMUM

  1. UNLESS UNAVOIDABLE, DO NOT CROSS THE PICKET LINE. JOIN IT
  1. Do not tell the administration whether your faculty are participating in the strike
  1. Email the school [template provided]
  1. Wear a button → actively show your support

IF YOU WANT TO GET MORE INVOLVED

  1. Get your parents to email the school

    [TNS takes their “source of income” much more seriously]

What can student workers do?

SENS members (student employees at The New School) cannot strike while covered by our current contract

  1. Sign the pledge refusing to fill in for striking part-time faculty
  1. Try to move your classes online
  1. Do not cross the picket line

if you still think that the strike is not necessary, know that this is the part-time faculty’s last resort. The bargaining with the administration has proven to be ineffective with the “final” offer by the university seeking to [source]:

  1. 2-2.5% annual raises for most faculty when inflation is nearing 9%
  1. Maintaining pay disparities between studio and lecture/seminar courses
  1. Continuing to drastically underpay Mannes prep faculty, other pre-college faculty, and Continuing Education faculty
  1. Tiered health insurance that cuts future private lesson instructors Mannes off from health insurance
  1. Higher health insurance premiums & ability to raise out-of-pocket insurance costs at any time
